# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Frequent and occasional travelers (25-45yo) who plan trips solo or with companions. Primary context: researching destinations on a laptop or phone, juggling flight/hotel/activity details, often feeling overwhelmed by fragmented tools. Job to be done: turn a vague travel idea into a concrete, shareable itinerary without cognitive overload.

## Product Purpose

TripPlanner is a collaborative trip planning platform that centralizes itinerary building, expense splitting, guide booking, and social discovery into one cohesive experience. Success means a trip that actually happens and everyone involved knows exactly where to be and what to pay.

## Brand Personality

**Adventurous & Warm** — Excited without being chaotic, inspiring without being preachy. The interface should feel like a helpful travel-savvy friend who keeps things organized so you can focus on the excitement. Tone: encouraging, practical, human. Never corporate-speak, never "enterprise software."

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI app look**: Avoid purple gradient everywhere, dark glassmorphism, frosted glass cards as decoration, identical card grids, hero-metric template layouts. These read as "AI-generated SaaS product" and fail the slop test.
- **Over-styled marketing surfaces**: The landing page earns its keep as an entry point, not the product. App UI takes absolute priority.
- **Booking.com density**: Too much information crammed into one view. TripPlanner shows what matters now; drill deeper when needed.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity at a glance**: Every screen answers "what should I do / see / decide next?" without scrolling.
2. **Progressive disclosure**: Simple by default, expandable for power users. Don't surface every option at once.
3. **Warmth through restraint**: Soft color palette with purposeful accent use. Not cold enterprise software.
4. **Planning should feel like anticipating the trip**: Use motion, imagery, and copy that evokes the excitement of travel, not the anxiety of data entry.
5. **Collaborative by default**: Every feature accounts for multiple people: travelers, guides, split payers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance minimum (4.5:1 contrast for body text)
- Focus states visible and styled, not browser defaults
- Keyboard navigable throughout
- Reduced motion respected for users who prefer it
